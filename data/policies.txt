SECTION 1: FACULTY WORKLOAD POLICIES
--------------------------------------

1.1 Maximum Weekly Teaching Load
- Maximum teaching hours per week for a Professor: 12 hours.
- Maximum teaching hours per week for an Associate Professor: 14 hours.
- Maximum teaching hours per week for an Assistant Professor: 16 hours.
- These limits include all lecture, tutorial, and laboratory sessions.
- Research and administrative duties are counted separately and do not reduce teaching load limits.

1.2 Minimum Weekly Teaching Load
- A full-time Professor must teach a minimum of 4 hours per week.
- A full-time Associate Professor must teach a minimum of 6 hours per week.
- A full-time Assistant Professor must teach a minimum of 8 hours per week.
- Faculty on sabbatical or medical leave are exempt from minimum load requirements.

1.3 Research Load
- Professors are expected to dedicate 4–6 hours per week to research activities.
- Associate Professors are expected to dedicate 2–4 hours per week to research activities.
- Assistant Professors are expected to dedicate 1–2 hours per week to research activities.
- Research load hours are tracked separately and do not count toward teaching hours.

1.4 Administrative Load
- HODs (Heads of Department) are allocated 2 administrative hours per week.
- Faculty serving on Senate, Academic Council, or major committees are allocated 1 additional administrative hour per week.
- Administrative load reduces maximum teaching availability by the same number of hours.

1.5 Overload Policy
- Faculty may be assigned overload only with written approval from the HOD and Dean.
- Overload teaching (beyond the maximum) must not exceed 4 additional hours per week.
- Overloaded faculty are entitled to additional remuneration as per the approved pay scale.
- Overload assignments must not persist for more than two consecutive semesters.

1.6 Guest / Visiting Faculty
- Visiting faculty must not be assigned more than 10 hours per week.
- Visiting faculty are not eligible for research or administrative load assignments.

SECTION 2: TIMETABLE SCHEDULING POLICIES
------------------------------------------

2.1 Consecutive Teaching Hours
- No faculty member shall have more than 3 consecutive teaching hours without a break.
- A minimum break of 1 hour (free slot) is mandatory between two consecutive teaching sessions exceeding 2 hours.
- Lab sessions of 2 hours are treated as a single block and count as 2 consecutive hours.

2.2 Daily Teaching Limits
- Maximum teaching hours in a single day for any faculty: 5 hours.
- Faculty should not be scheduled for sessions before 08:00 AM or after 06:00 PM.
- At least one day per week must have no teaching assignment (faculty rest day).

2.3 Free Slot Requirements
- All faculty must have at least one completely free slot (1 hour) between any two sessions on the same day.
- No faculty should be scheduled in the last slot (16:00–17:00) more than 3 days per week.
- Friday afternoon (after 15:00) sessions should be minimized and only used when no other option exists.

2.4 Room and Resource Allocation
- A single room cannot be assigned to two different sessions at the same time.
- Lab sessions require dedicated lab rooms (Lab 101–Lab 502); regular classrooms cannot be used for labs.
- Room capacity must not be exceeded; enrollment above 90% of capacity requires room reassignment.
- Smart/seminar rooms (capacity > 80) are to be used only for seminars, workshops, or merged sections.

2.5 Lunch Break Policy
- No sessions shall be scheduled between 13:00 and 14:00 (designated lunch break).
- This slot is reserved for faculty meetings, student consultation, and departmental activities.

2.6 Session Clash Policy
- A faculty member cannot be assigned to two sessions at the same time slot.
- A student section (e.g., CSE Sem 3 Section A) cannot have two courses scheduled at the same time.
- In case of detected clashes, the timetable must be revised within 48 hours of identification.

SECTION 3: DEPARTMENT-SPECIFIC RULES
---------------------------------------

3.1 Computer Science & Engineering (CSE)
- All CSE lab sessions must be conducted in designated CS labs (Lab 101–Lab 209).
- Programming-based courses (CS201, CS302, CS304) must have one lab session per week in addition to lectures.
- Final-year project (CS499) faculty guides are not counted in regular teaching load.
- Maximum number of courses a CSE faculty can handle simultaneously: 3 courses.

3.2 Electrical & Electronics Engineering (EEE)
- High-voltage lab sessions (EE201, EE303) require a co-supervisor in addition to the faculty-in-charge.
- EEE lab sessions must not be scheduled after 16:00 due to safety regulations.
- Faculty teaching power systems courses must hold a valid electrical safety certification.

3.3 Mechanical Engineering (ME)
- Workshop and manufacturing lab sessions (ME303, ME302) require mandatory safety induction before first session.
- Heavy machinery labs must always have a lab technician present alongside the faculty.
- ME faculty must submit lab session risk assessments at the start of each semester.

3.4 Civil Engineering (CIVIL)
- Field survey sessions (CE101) count as 2 hours regardless of actual duration for load calculation purposes.
- Site visit sessions must be pre-approved by the HOD at least 1 week in advance.
- CE faculty conducting site visits are exempt from campus sessions on the same day.

SECTION 4: SUBSTITUTION AND LEAVE POLICIES
--------------------------------------------

4.1 Planned Leave
- Faculty planning leave must notify the HOD at least 5 working days in advance.
- For leaves longer than 3 days, a substitute faculty must be arranged by the concerned department.
- Substitute faculty must be from the same department and must have relevant subject expertise.

4.2 Emergency Leave
- In case of emergency leave, the HOD must be informed within 2 hours of the missed session.
- The department is responsible for arranging a substitute within 24 hours.
- Students must be notified via the official LMS (Learning Management System) about any session cancellation.

4.3 Substitution Limits
- A faculty member shall not be assigned substitution duties that raise their total load beyond the overload threshold.
- Substitution for more than 3 sessions per semester requires approval from the Dean of Academics.

---

SECTION 5: EXAMINATION AND EVALUATION DUTIES
----------------------------------------------

5.1 Invigilation Duties
- Every faculty member is expected to perform a minimum of 4 invigilation duties per semester.
- HODs and faculty with administrative loads above 2 hours/week are exempt from invigilation duties.
- Faculty on medical leave or maternity/paternity leave are exempt during the leave period.

5.2 Paper Setting and Evaluation
- Course-handling faculty are responsible for setting end-semester examination papers.
- Paper moderation must be completed at least 2 weeks before the examination date.
- Answer script evaluation must be completed within 10 working days of the examination.

---

SECTION 6: WORKLOAD REPORTING
-------------------------------

6.1 Semester Reports
- All faculty must submit a workload self-declaration form at the beginning of each semester.
- HODs must submit consolidated department workload reports to the Dean by the 3rd week of each semester.
- Discrepancies between declared and actual workload must be resolved within 1 week of identification.

6.2 Annual Review
- Faculty workload compliance is reviewed annually by the Academic Council.
- Persistent overload or underload situations are flagged for corrective action.
- The timetable committee must ensure equitable distribution of workload across departments.

6.3 Workload Audit
- An independent workload audit is conducted once per academic year.
- Faculty with consistently high or low loads are reviewed for structural reallocation.
- Audit findings are shared with the Academic Senate for policy revisions.

---

SECTION 7: DEFINITIONS
------------------------

- Teaching Hour: One scheduled 60-minute session of lecture, tutorial, or laboratory instruction.
- Free Slot: A 60-minute period with no scheduled teaching or examination duty.
- Consecutive Hours: Teaching sessions scheduled back-to-back with no free slot in between.
- Overload: Teaching load exceeding the maximum prescribed for a given designation.
- Session Clash: Two sessions assigned to the same faculty, room, or student section at the same time.
- Research Load: Time formally allocated for research, publication, and project supervision.
- Administrative Load: Time allocated for departmental or institutional administrative responsibilities.
